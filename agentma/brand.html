agentma is built around a single drawing — a hand-sketched pumpkin-headed creature — and a rule: nothing about the brand looks machine-rendered. Real ink. Real paper. Real teeth. Use this book to keep it that way.

✓do
  • Keep the wobble. The lines are hand-drawn — let them be hand-drawn.
  • Use the cream paper or solid ink as the ground. Both shipped with the system.
  • Pair with a soft underline scribble when the wordmark needs to feel "alive".
  • Shrink the head-only lockup to 32px for favicons. The teeth still read.
✗don't
  • Don't re-trace it in Illustrator. The bumps and gaps in the strokes are the brand.
  • Don't add a drop shadow, glow, gradient, or bevel. Flat ink only.
  • Don't rotate the creature past ±5° — it stops feeling like a character and starts feeling like a meme.
  • Don't change the colors. Maroon on cream, cream on ink. Orange is an accent, not a fill.

04voice

Confident, slightly haunted, never corporate. Talks to engineers who'd rather skim than be sold to. Short sentences. Lowercase nav. Em-dashes earn their place.

SAY
"wrangle your agents before they wrangle you."
Active. Threatens just enough to be funny. Names the problem in 7 words.
DON'T SAY
"Empowering enterprise AI orchestration."
Three words you'd find on a slide. No specifics. No personality. No teeth.
SAY
"this one tried to call production stripe at 3am."
Concrete moment. Specific time. Specific tool. Reads like a real Slack message.
DON'T SAY
"Anomalous behavior detected in your AI workflows."
Abstract, passive, jargon. Could be any vendor. Could be a fire alarm.
